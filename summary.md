# Book 1 Chapter Summaries

## Chapter 1: Things set in motion
Leo Turner convinces his best friend Ian Hagen to hire Jay Hall as his new assistant, despite Ian's deep mistrust stemming from a past betrayal by a corporate spy posing as his secretary. Jay arrives desperate for a safe workplace after fleeing sexual harassment at his previous job, seeing Ian's legendary coldness toward office romance as protection. While Jay performs excellently and genuinely appreciates the opportunity, Ian remains suspicious, unable to distinguish between Jay's authentic earnestness and potential manipulation.

## Chapter 2: Fitting in
Jay's solitary weekend routine reveals his disciplined, isolated life as he prepares meticulously for work. He begins joining team lunches despite the financial strain and social exhaustion, struggling to perform normalcy. When Jay makes a spreadsheet error, Ian helps him fix it with unexpected patience, confusing Jay's understanding of their dynamic. Despite Jay's competent work and the team's genuine warmth, Ian's suspicions deepen—seeing Jay's very lack of agenda as potentially the most sophisticated manipulation of all.

## Chapter 3: Establishing himself
Jay settles into office life, discovering small comforts like a favorite bistro and a pocket park. He contributes valuable insight during a strategy meeting about the Wentworth client, earning Ian's rare praise. After successfully attending Friday drinks and completing his first month, Jay discovers his compensation includes substantial bonuses he hadn't expected. The financial security is overwhelming, but he remains cautious, knowing his three-month probation isn't over. Ian continues to observe him carefully, caught between growing respect for Jay's work and persistent fear of being deceived again.

## Chapter 4: The ripples
Ian's attraction to and respect for Jay creates dangerous echoes of his past with Sam Connelly. When Jay asks about a previous acquisition, Ian shuts him down harshly, creating painful distance between them. Jay withdraws completely, becoming invisible and resigned. The return of Max, a boisterous senior analyst, shatters Jay's fragile peace when he casually refers to Jay as Leo's "joke hire." Jay discovers online the full story of Ian's scandal and the speculation about his "type" in hiring. Realizing he may be a pawn in Leo's games, Jay feels betrayed but trapped—this exploitative job is still safer than his previous one.

## Chapter 5: Finding his place
At the lavish quarterly party, Jay feels like an imposter despite his expensive suit, watching Ian's warmth with others while receiving only cold courtesy himself. Ian assigns Jay an important compliance audit, attempting to show his value, but Jay processes it mechanically. During the celebration of the Hartwell acquisition, Max makes an inappropriate comment about Jay's appearance helping win the client. Though Max later apologizes and Jay receives his permanent contract with a raise, he feels nothing. The achievement tastes like ash—he's earned security by staying invisible, trading dignity for safety. Three months in, Jay exists in what he describes as "a comfortable prison," slowly rotting inside a respectable tomb.

## Chapter 6: Regaining ground
At Friday drinks celebrating Jay's permanent status, Leo drives Jay home and explains that while appearance factored into hiring, Jay's competence has genuinely impressed everyone. Ian, observing Jay's withdrawn state, realizes his paranoia was unfounded—Jay is simply a wounded young man trying to survive. During a late-night crisis with the Fairfield account, Ian patiently helps Jay through a problem, and Jay begins to trust again. The warmth in their brief working moments starts to thaw the ice between them, with Jay tentatively believing he might actually belong at Turner & Associates.

## Chapter 7: A sense of security
After successfully handling the Fairfield crisis, Jay spends his afternoon reading at a café, finally feeling secure enough to enjoy simple pleasures. He calls his mother about his new job, attends The Palladium dinner where Ian protectively shields him from uncomfortable attention, and starts to see Ian's formal distance not as rejection but as professional integrity. Jay realizes Ian's leadership creates the safe environment he's always needed. Meanwhile, Ian throws himself into the Hartwell acquisition to distract from his growing feelings for Jay, working punishing hours through late November. Jay notices Ian's exhaustion and quietly streamlines his workload, offering unobtrusive support that Ian deeply appreciates despite his attempts at emotional distance.

## Chapter 8: The festive season
December brings Christmas decorating, with Jay working closely with Ian on the office tree and buying his first personal decorations. He orders an expensive custom tuxedo for the upcoming gala and sends thoughtful gifts to his estranged family, choosing to stay in the city for Christmas. At the Christmas party, Leo casually mentions Ian's isolation, making Jay concerned. Jay spends Christmas alone but content, while Ian works through the holiday in his empty penthouse. On Christmas night, Ian dreams vividly of kissing Jay in his office, waking to profound loneliness and rage at Leo for creating this situation. The next morning, he drags Leo to freezing tennis courts and brutally destroys him for three hours, though Leo correctly identifies that Ian is attracted to Jay and terrified of it.

## Chapter 9: New Year
During the quiet week between Christmas and New Year, only Jay, Luc, Ela, and Ian remain working. When Ela gets sick, it's just the three of them. Jay works late helping Ian, and in the empty office after Luc leaves, experiences a charged moment of intimacy—realizing he trusts Ian completely, perhaps more than he's ever trusted anyone. On New Year's Eve, Ian surprises them by sharing his best whiskey and opening up slightly. Jay receives a massive year-end bonus of $80,000 and uses it to buy three quality suits. He declines Leo's attempts to get him to socialize at bars, preferring quiet evenings. As he prepares for the upcoming gala, purchasing a custom tuxedo and getting an expensive haircut, Jay realizes his life has fundamentally changed from the scared, broke man who started six months ago.

## Chapter 10: The high finance
At the prestigious gala, Jay looks stunning in his tuxedo. Ian can barely control his reaction, and Leo teases him about his jealousy when other men notice Jay. During the evening, a predatory client named Cruver corners Jay, making inappropriate advances and touching him despite Jay's discomfort. Jay flees to the bathroom in tears, feeling violated and ashamed. Ian finds him and, seeing his distress, immediately arranges to leave with him. In Ian's car and later at Jay's modest apartment over tea, Ian gently helps Jay process the trauma with humor and kindness. Their connection deepens, though both remain in denial about romantic feelings. Ian secretly begins planning retribution against Cruver. When Ian offers Jay a promotion to analyst, Jay shocks everyone by declining—he wants to stay as Ian's assistant, unwilling to leave the close working relationship they've developed. His choice reveals his true feelings, even if he can't fully acknowledge them yet.

## Chapter 11: February rush
February brings brutal cold and heavy strategy season workload. Ian gives Jay the company card for team expenses, and Jay orders an elaborate dinner that lifts spirits during stressful periods. HR announces retreat room assignments—Leo manipulates the situation so Ian and Jay will share a room, claiming team integration. Despite Ian's protests and Jay's discomfort about being "unworthy" of sharing with the CFO, the arrangement is finalized. Leo later tells Ian that Jay needs someone safe in his corner, and Ian's resistance crumbles under his own protective instincts.

## Chapter 12: Stormy weather
A violent winter storm leaves Jay soaking wet and forty-five minutes late to work. He buys an emergency designer outfit from Hathaway's Menswear that makes him look stunning, and Ian struggles to control his reaction. Days later, Jay falls seriously ill with the flu and spends a week alone and feverish in his apartment, his work phone dying. Ian becomes increasingly worried as days pass without word, checking his phone constantly and imagining worst-case scenarios. When Jay finally recovers enough to text that he'll return Monday, Ian feels overwhelming relief.

## Chapter 13: Ides of March
Jay makes a serious mistake by misfiling an important legal email, causing them to miss a compliance deadline. Ian handles it professionally, but Jay spirals into self-doubt until Luc takes him for hot chocolate with rum and shares his own early mistake story. Meanwhile, Ian's three-month investigation into Orrey Capital Partners reaches fruition—he's uncovered serious corruption and regulatory violations. Turner & Associates terminates the relationship, and when the news breaks publicly, Jay feels vindictive satisfaction knowing Cruver's employer is facing federal investigation and he'll never see him at company events again.

## Chapter 14: Spring
April's crushing quarterly reporting deadlines consume the office with extended hours and weekend work, finally making Jay understand why the bonuses are so generous. Ian gives Jay a generous care package after his illness—ginger tea, vitamins, supplements—claiming it's "standard procedure" though everyone knows it isn't. As spring arrives, Jay resumes gym sessions, explores the city, and in a spontaneous gesture uses the company card to buy specialty coffee for the entire team. Ian drinks the maple bourbon latte Jay selected for him despite it being nothing like his usual preference, touched by the thoughtfulness.

## Chapter 15: Inferiority
Jay spends Easter weekend alone studying advanced economics texts, anxious about his credentials compared to his accomplished colleagues. One evening after everyone leaves, Ian discovers Jay's economics textbook and begins reading his margin notes—annotations revealing Jay's intellectual curiosity, humility, and careful engagement with complex material. Ian spends over an hour reading Jay's private study notes, discovering one mentions Ian by name as Jay tries to understand the practical wisdom Ian applies. Sitting in Jay's chair surrounded by evidence of his self-directed education, Ian acknowledges his feelings have grown beyond professional respect into dangerous territory.

## Chapter 16: Preparations
Ian takes Jay to a client meeting with the Calder Wetherby Trust, driving through affluent neighborhoods that fascinate Jay. Afterward they share an expensive lunch where Ian opens up about his privileged upbringing, creating unexpected intimacy. During the traffic-delayed return, Jay becomes acutely aware of Ian's cologne and presence. That Friday, Jay finally accepts Luc's invitation to drinks and learns how much preparation the retreat requires. He embarks on an exhausting shopping expedition through the fashion district, spending months of salary on resort wear, then returns to Hathaway's for an expensive evening outfit.

## Chapter 17: Retreat
The three-day company retreat at a luxurious beachfront resort fundamentally changes Jay and Ian's relationship. They share an elegant suite, and the first night Jay gets pleasantly drunk at the spectacular sunset dinner while Ian monitors him protectively. The second day includes scuba diving—Jay's first time—under Ian's patient guidance, the underwater world breathtaking. Evening conversations by the bonfire, morning coffee together, the domesticity of sharing a room—all create an intimacy neither expected. Ian's puzzle-solving brilliance wins them concert tickets in the treasure hunt, and both men return to the city changed by the experience.

## Chapter 18: Artistic merits
Ian helps Jay take professional office photos for Ema, including a candid selfie together that Jay treasures. The team attends the concert they won—experimental electronic music at an underground club. Jay and Ian escape to the bar, bonding over their shared bewilderment at the incomprehensible performance, discovering mutual lack of appreciation for avant-garde art. Ian reveals he played violin seriously through college. The disaster of a concert becomes enjoyable through their companionship, and Ian jokes about finding better whiskey next time.

## Chapter 19: Family
Jay reorganizes the filing system and expands his knowledge during June's slower season, then drives the company limousine to Ema's graduation. The luxury vehicle creates complications—her classmates swarm for photos and Ema introduces her "investment banker brother." Jay completely overshadows Ema's achievement despite trying to downplay his success. Back at the office, Jay displays a family photo on his desk, and Ian asks unexpectedly personal questions about the visit. Jay refuses to take vacation unless Ian does, creating an impasse neither can resolve.

## Chapter 20: Milestones
Jay turns thirty and achieves his goal of being completely debt-free. Ian spends the day debating whether to acknowledge Jay's birthday, ultimately staying silent. Monday's annual review brings Ian offering the analyst position again—Jay refuses immediately, wanting to stay as Ian's assistant. Ian gives Jay an extremely expensive watch as a one-year anniversary gift and apology for all the ways he failed Jay during his first year. The intensity makes Jay uncomfortable, but he accepts the watch that fits perfectly. That evening, Ian finally admits to himself: he's in love with Jay, hopelessly and impossibly, and Jay's innocent admiration shows no awareness of Ian as anything more than a mentor.

# Book 2 Chapter Summaries

## Chapter 21: Summer heat
Ian realizes he's in love with Jay and decides to manage his feelings appropriately while ensuring Jay's happiness. When Ian skips lunch, Jay notices and orders soup for him, showing unexpected attentiveness that makes Ian's feelings deepen. Their dynamic shifts as Jay grows comfortable offering care, while Ian struggles between wanting Jay close and fearing his own inappropriate feelings. The summer heat mirrors their growing but unspoken connection.

## Chapter 22: Schemer and imp
Jay panics about his upcoming diving trip, having never flown before. Ian mysteriously arranges a business trip to the New Continent, claiming it's for training but clearly designed to ease Jay's travel anxiety. Leo visits Ian and teases him mercilessly about his feelings for Jay, while separately cornering Jay to warn him about being gentle with Ian's heart. Leo arranges expensive tailored suits for Jay through a condescending upscale tailor, leaving Jay feeling humiliated and manipulated, though Ian insists Leo means well despite his meddling ways.

## Chapter 23: Fragility
September brings Jay's wounded pride from the tailor experience, making him withdraw into his normal life—gym sessions, café visits, swimming. He establishes boundaries, leaving work at five and reclaiming his independence, which devastates Ian who misses their evening conversations. When Ian confronts Jay about his distance, Jay admits to having "a rough weekend" but won't elaborate. Luc's sudden breakup after eight years with Cam leads to an emotional sleepover where Jay and Luc bond over shared heartbreak and terrible scrambled eggs.

## Chapter 24: Business trip
Ian and Jay travel together for the Garnet Ridge Group meeting, with Ian carefully managing every detail to ease Jay's first international experience. A business dinner goes wrong when an executive makes inappropriate comments about Jay, prompting Ian to terminate the entire partnership. Ian takes Jay shopping for his siblings and to a spectacular restaurant, transforming what could have been a disaster into a protective, intimate experience. Flight delays on the return create more shared time, with Ian offering wisdom about Jay's worth regardless of others' treatment, and Jay realizing he wants to be closer to Ian—not just as employee and boss, but as something more meaningful.

## Chapter 25: Tropical heat
Jay's solo diving vacation in Lagoon Belt proves he can handle independence, earning his open water certification while enjoying solitary beach time. The quarterly reports immediately follow, consuming everyone in brutal overtime that leaves the team exhausted. Ian had looked forward to their late-night work sessions but finds Jay constantly pulled in different directions by colleagues needing help. The quarterly party is subdued, everyone too drained to celebrate properly. Jay begins apartment hunting seriously, overwhelmed by options and unable to find anything that feels right.

## Chapter 26: Uncharted territories
Jay returns from vacation glowing with confidence, and their connection deepens through extended evening conversations. Jay organizes a fencing night for Luc's benefit, recruiting his gym trainer Rus and friends, which becomes a chaotic but successful team competition despite Jay losing every match. Luc pressures Jay into a blind date that goes disastrously—an arrogant man named Yan mocks Jay as a "funeral director" and spills wine on his best suit. Jay calls Ian for rescue, leading to Ian staying overnight at Jay's cramped studio, making breakfast, and sharing his story of living in poverty for eight years after being disowned.

## Chapter 27: Shared traumas
The disastrous blind date ends with Jay drunk and Ian staying over to care for him. The next morning, their conversation about accepting help leads to Ian revealing his complete destitution after being disowned—selling his watch, living in a worse studio than Jay's, surviving on nothing. When Ian insists he "deserved" his family's rejection, Jay angrily defends him and shares his own trauma of sexual harassment at his first job. They exchange their deepest wounds, with Jay physically embracing Ian and telling him firmly "it was not your fault." The intimacy of shared vulnerability fundamentally changes their relationship.

## Chapter 28: Unusually warm autumn
Jay impulsively invites himself to Ian's apartment to help with weekend work, leading to an unexpectedly domestic Saturday of spreadsheets, homemade pasta, and easy companionship. Jay then plans a perfect Sunday date—ferry ride along the river, seasonal pumpkin restaurant, botanical gardens where they feed ducks together. Ian reveals he did nothing for fun during his eight years of poverty, and Jay vows to ensure Ian takes proper breaks. Despite the perfect day, Monday brings heavy melancholy as they return to their work roles, both feeling the painful distance between what was and what can never be.

## Chapter 29: Choices
Ian manipulates a real estate agent to show Jay a suspiciously perfect apartment near Ian's own, hoping to engineer proximity. Jay suspects conspiracy but everyone encourages him to take the opportunity. Luc organizes another social disaster at laser tag that leaves Jay muddy and miserable. The Christmas tree decorating tradition separates Jay and Ian to different teams, but Ian secretly orders a small tree for his office so they can decorate together privately. They make plans to spend Christmas together, and Jay decides to finally commit to buying the apartment, ready to move forward despite his fears.

## Chapter 30: Winter lights
The week between Christmas and New Year becomes an intimate bubble as Jay and Ian work closely with skeleton crew, sharing lunches and long evening conversations. Jay spends Christmas day at Ian's apartment—they decorate a tree, bake together in matching aprons, exchange meaningful gifts (Jay gives Ian an engraved lighter, Ian gives Jay sapphire cufflinks). The January reporting period is brutal, but Jay promises Ian another outing once it's over. At the gala, Ian is rattled by a nightmare of Jay dying and must cancel their plans for emergency travel. When Ian returns Jay to his apartment and drinks heavily, he leaves with the words "You looked beautiful tonight," leaving both of them unsettled by the implications.

## Chapter 31: Things that slip through fingers
February floods devastate the metro system and derail apartment viewings. Jay accidentally drops a critical database table at work, panicking until Ian calmly restores it from backup. The emergency fund envelope containing $10,000 cash disappears from Jay's apartment, likely stolen during building chaos. Ian notices Jay's distress and offers help, but Jay refuses to explain, ashamed of losing so much money through carelessness. Ian worries about Jay constantly, noticing his withdrawn mood and inability to focus.

## Chapter 32: Alternatives
Desperate after losing his emergency fund, Jay secretly sends out 50+ job applications and secures an interview with Accord Financial Services offering a significant raise. He tours the sterile business district, evaluating whether he could leave Ian's side for financial security. After the interview, Jay gets drunk with Rus and friends who diagnose him as "in love" with his boss—a realization Jay refuses to accept. He wrestles with the choice between safety and staying near the person who's become the center of his world.

## Chapter 33: What's worth fighting for
Jay faces his second interview with Accord Financial Services while Ian watches him on the phone, panicking that Jay might be leaving. Jay ultimately declines their generous offer, choosing to stay with Ian despite the lost emergency fund. That same afternoon, Ian conducts Jay's performance review and offers a massive raise plus promotion to Senior Assistant—far more than Accord offered. Ian had been terrified of losing Jay and overcompensates with an almost desperate compensation package. Jay accepts, relieved he made the right choice, while Ian privately acknowledges he would have offered anything to keep Jay close.

## Chapter 34: Damp weather
March brings recovery from February's chaos as relationships normalize. Willow and Sage invite Jay to lunch, and he signs up for the spring retreat. Ian helps Jay navigate mortgage paperwork for the apartment purchase. Jay's family visits for Easter, requiring extensive hosting that exhausts him. The quarterly party arrives with the team in better spirits. Throughout it all, Jay and Ian settle into a comfortable rhythm of work and quiet domesticity, though neither acknowledges the growing tension beneath the surface.

## Chapter 35: Home
Jay moves into his new apartment with help from the team, transforming the space with his belongings and hosting a housewarming party. Ian brings an expensive coffee machine as a gift and lingers after others leave, helping clean up. In a moment of significance, Jay gives Ian a spare key "for emergencies," creating a new level of access and trust between them. The apartment becomes a symbol of Jay's stability and success, while also placing him walking distance from Ian's penthouse—a proximity both thrilling and dangerous for their unacknowledged feelings.

## Chapter 36: Foolish fantasies
Ray Shore joins the team as a new analyst, disrupting the established dynamic. Jay trains Ray while struggling with his growing awareness of his feelings for Ian. During a particularly difficult day, Jay catches himself having romantic fantasies about Ian and immediately suppresses them, horrified at the inappropriateness. Ian notices Jay's distraction and assumes he's upset about something at work. The chapter ends with both men trapped in their separate denials, each convinced their feelings are impossible and unwelcome.

## Chapter 37: Thunderstorm
The spring company retreat brings forced proximity when Jay and Ian share a suite again. An evening scent-making workshop leads to absinthe drinking and philosophical conversations about desire and fear. Jay gets thoroughly drunk and Ian helps him to bed, staying to ensure he's safe. Jay wakes up in Ian's bed the next morning with fragmented memories of being carried and cared for, feeling both mortified and strangely cherished. The retreat crystallizes the impossibility of their situation—so close yet unable to cross the final boundary separating them.

## Chapter 38: New faces
May brings multiple new hires to Turner & Associates, changing the office dynamic as the team expands. Gal, a charismatic new senior analyst, begins monopolizing Jay's time with constant questions and requests for help. Jay, too kind to refuse, finds himself increasingly pulled away from Ian by Gal's demands. Ian watches with growing frustration as Gal becomes clingy and possessive of Jay's attention. The team notices the tension but misreads it as normal workplace dynamics rather than Ian's poorly concealed jealousy.

## Chapter 39: Facing the truth
After weeks of watching Gal monopolize Jay's time, Ian finally confronts his own feelings during a late-night work session. Alone in his office, watching Jay through the glass wall as he patiently helps Gal yet again, Ian can no longer deny the truth: he's desperately, completely in love with Jay. The realization is both liberating and devastating—he understands his feelings with perfect clarity but also knows he can never act on them. Jay's trust, their working relationship, the power imbalance, all make confession impossible. Ian must choose between having Jay as his assistant or losing him entirely.

## Chapter 40: The storm before the calm
Jay's family visits for the weekend—mother Ira and siblings Ema, Tim, and Ari tour the city and his office. Leo and Luc organize Jay's birthday party at The Palladium; Ira is protective and wary of Ian, not trusting "rich, powerful men." Ian gives Jay onyx cufflinks, fastening them onto Jay in an achingly intimate moment. After the family leaves, Gal corners Jay in the archive room and files a false assault claim when rebuffed—surveillance footage proves Jay's innocence and she's terminated immediately. Jay spirals with guilt; Leo visits revealing Ian faced similar accusations years ago. Days later, Ian appears at Jay's apartment, apologizes for not acting sooner, and pulls Jay into a desperate embrace. Jay asks if they can still do their cancelled winter outing "someday." They separate, both leaning against opposite sides of the same wall, longing for each other. It's June—summer has arrived.

# Book 3 Chapter Summaries

## Chapter 41: New traditions
Jay marks his two-year anniversary at Turner & Associates, reflecting on how far he's come. With Gal gone, the office feels safe again. Ian takes Jay to dinner to thank him for staying despite all the difficulties, and their companionship deepens. Leo arrives with plans for Ian's 40th birthday party, and Jay reluctantly agrees to help gauge Ian's interest. Jay begins making homemade tinctures as Ian's birthday gift, anxiously comparing his modest present to whatever extravagant gift Leo might find. Ian orders a small office Christmas tree specifically so he can decorate it with Jay. They establish new traditions—Tuesday evening runs, Friday dinners, plans for Christmas together—each thread weaving their lives more tightly.

## Chapter 42: Invitations
Leo's party planning spirals out of control—he invites Ema, Jay's sister, along with Rus and his gym friends, even Eli the stylist. Ema desperately begs to attend, leading to tense negotiations with Jay's protective mother. Jay seeks help from Eli for a daring, stunning outfit for the party. The car search for Tim's 18th birthday drags on, adding stress. Ian becomes quietly jealous when Jay mentions inviting "an acquaintance" (Eli) to the party, not realizing it's innocent gratitude. September brings new analyst Tia Larson, who integrates smoothly. Jay and Ian continue their deepening routine of dinners and runs, though Ian wrestles with possessive feelings he knows he shouldn't have.

## Chapter 43: The celebration
Ian's 40th birthday gala turns out to be a spectacular event with 500 guests—politicians, artists, old friends, and flamboyantly queer crowds—not the small gathering promised. Jay arrives late, breathtaking in his floral blazer, and wishes Ian happy birthday with heartfelt words about how much Ian matters. Ema and Lam attend in dramatic Victorian gowns, supervised by Rus and Van. A guest mistakes Jay for Ian's boyfriend, leading to awkward corrections. The party features fireworks, a vaudeville star, and an orchestra. Ian opens gifts later—Jay's homemade tinctures are his absolute favorite, carefully crafted over months. Leo's gift is a hideous antique brooch that makes Jay laugh with relief. Ian treasures Jay's thoughtful, personal gift above all others.

## Chapter 44: Afterglow
Jay apologizes for the party's excess and helps Ian sort through the mountain of birthday gifts. They discover Ema's gift—tickets to a spring botanical garden—which Ian eagerly accepts as an excuse for a weekend trip. They plan a November vacation to the Songbird Islands, ten days at a luxury resort. The quarterly reports pass with unprecedented ease thanks to Ray and Liv's efficiency. Jay successfully purchases a reliable car for Tim's 18th birthday. However, with reporting season over, Jay feels an aching melancholy—the Friday dinners have stopped, the runs are paused for winter, and despite seeing Ian daily, he feels desperately lonely. Both men are quietly pining, missing the intimacy of their established routines.

## Chapter 45: Steam and stars
The team travels to a northern hot springs resort for the promised weekend retreat. Jay nearly freezes on a hiking trip, having forgotten thermal underwear, but recovers in the healing pools. That evening they witness a spectacular aurora borealis from an ocean-edge pool where hot spring water meets freezing ocean. Later, at a naked sauna session, Jay is overwhelmed seeing Ian's body. At dinner, Jay gets drunk and outside in the cold, clings to Ian desperately, eventually begging him to "hold me for the rest of my life." Ian complies, holding Jay as he sleeps, while suffering the devastating impossibility of their situation. Jay wakes mortified with memory gaps, apologizing profusely. Ian pretends nothing happened to spare Jay embarrassment, while internally falling apart from the cruelty of Jay's drunken plea revealing exactly what Ian wants but can never have.
