# Clouds Over Gethsemane — Chapter Summaries

*Clouds Over Gethsemane is a slowburn BL romance set in high-stakes finance — the
gradual, unspoken pull between **Ian Hagen**, a guarded CFO scarred by an old
betrayal, and **Jay Hall**, the assistant who joins his team fleeing harassment.
It is a story told through accumulated moments rather than dramatic declarations.*

*Derived from the scene-by-scene, dated chronologies in `timeline/`, which hold
the detail and the dates. This file is the brief, pasteable overview; further
books are added as their timelines are built.*

## Book 1 — Jay's first year

*From Jay's desperate arrival at Turner & Associates to the night Ian privately
admits he is in love. Across the year Ian moves from paranoid suspicion to
acknowledged-but-hopeless love; Jay, fleeing a guarded boss famous for his
coldness toward romance, finds belonging and feelings he can't yet name —
declining a promotion at year's end to stay exactly where he is. (Aug 2016 – Aug
2017. Condensed from `timeline/book1-timeline.md`.)*

### Chapter 1: Things set in motion
Leo Turner pressures his best friend Ian into hiring Jay as his assistant, reopening the wound of Ian's betrayal by Sam Connelly, a corporate spy who once posed as his secretary. Jay, fleeing sexual harassment at his old job, is privately relieved to land under a boss famous for his coldness toward office romance. Jay's earnest competence only deepens Ian's suspicion — to him it reads as exactly what a skilled manipulator would project.

### Chapter 2: Fitting in
Jay's disciplined, isolated weekends and his exhausting effort to "perform normalcy" at team lunches establish who he is. When he makes a small error in his projections, Ian sits down and patiently walks him through the fix instead of scolding him, confusing Jay's sense of their dynamic. Drinking with Leo afterward, Ian refuses to soften — some lessons are too expensive to learn twice.

### Chapter 3: Establishing himself
Settling in, Jay earns Ian's rare praise by reframing an anxious client's emails during a strategy session. He survives his first Friday drinks and completes his first month, stunned to find his pay includes a large standard bonus — which he quietly banks, mindful that two months of probation remain.

### Chapter 4: The ripples
Ian shuts Jay down coldly when he asks about an old acquisition, and Jay retreats into deliberate invisibility. Max returns and loudly greets Jay as "Leo's joke hire"; that night Jay finds the old espionage scandal and the gossip about Ian's "type" online, and his safe harbor begins to feel false — though still safer than where he came from.

### Chapter 5: Finding his place
At his first lavish quarterly party Jay feels like an impostor, given only cold courtesy by Ian. Ian tries to signal Jay's worth by handing him a sensitive compliance audit, which Jay delivers superbly but mechanically. Max apologizes for the "joke hire" jab, and at his three-month review Jay accepts a permanent contract — feeling hollow, his security bought by staying invisible.

### Chapter 6: Regaining ground
Driving Jay home after the celebration, Leo admits appearance factored into the hire but insists Jay has earned his place on merit. Ian, reviewing a spotless background check, finally accepts that Jay is simply a wounded young man and resolves to become the fair mentor he needs. A late-night Fairfield crisis worked through together begins to thaw the ice between them.

### Chapter 7: A sense of security
Jay starts to read Ian's formal distance as professional integrity rather than rejection — the safe workplace he's always needed. At the Hartwell celebration dinner at The Palladium, Ian deftly shields him from Turner Senior's probing. Meanwhile Ian buries himself in punishing work to smother his growing feelings, and Jay quietly streamlines his load without being asked.

### Chapter 8: The festive season
December brings tree-decorating beside Ian, Jay's first personal decorations, and a custom tuxedo ordered for the gala; he ships gifts to his family and chooses to stay in the city for Christmas. Leo's remark about Ian's lonely, family-less holidays leaves Jay worried for him. On Christmas night Ian wakes from a vivid dream of kissing Jay and, rattled, drags Leo to dawn tennis and brutalizes him for hours — while Leo names the fear out loud: Ian wants Jay and is terrified of it.

### Chapter 9: New Year
Over the quiet skeleton-crew week between the holidays, Jay realizes how completely he trusts Ian, and Ian shares his best whiskey in a rare show of warmth. Jay's $80,000 year-end bonus — more than he has ever held at once — goes toward three tailored suits. He readies himself for the gala with an expensive haircut and culls the cheap interview suit he first arrived in.

### Chapter 10: The high finance
At the black-tie gala Jay looks striking and Ian, visibly affected, abandons networking to hover protectively. Separated from him, Jay is cornered and groped by a predatory client, Cruver; Ian finds him in tears, slips them out a back exit, and gently defuses the trauma over tea at Jay's apartment — secretly resolving to make Cruver pay. At his six-month review Jay shocks everyone by declining a promotion to analyst, choosing to stay as Ian's assistant — his true feelings showing in a choice he can't yet name.

### Chapter 11: February rush
Strategy season peaks and Jay becomes newly, physically aware of Ian in close quarters. Ian hands him a company credit card, and Jay uses it to lift the exhausted team's morale with an elaborate dinner that even draws Ian into the kitchen. HR's notices about the May retreat begin surfacing in the background — something Jay isn't ready to contemplate.

### Chapter 12: Stormy weather
A violent ice storm leaves Jay drenched and late for the first time; he buys a designer outfit from Hathaway's, not realizing how it unsettles Ian. Days later a severe flu fells him — bedridden and alone for over a week with his work phone dead — while Ian grows frantic at the silence. Jay returns pale and thin to a lavish "standard procedure" care package that warms him even as the illness leaves him freshly aware of his isolation.

### Chapter 13: Ides of March
Jay misfiles a legal notice and misses a compliance deadline; Ian corrects him gently, but Jay spirals until Luc takes him for spiked hot chocolate and shares his own rookie disaster. Meanwhile Ian's months-long investigation into Orrey Capital Partners — Cruver's employer — comes to fruition. Orrey is terminated amid a federal investigation, and Jay feels vindictive relief that he'll never face Cruver again.

### Chapter 14: Spring
April's first quarterly crunch — seven-day weeks, midnight finishes — finally teaches Jay why the bonuses are so generous. Leo engineers the May retreat's "mentor" rooming so that Ian and Jay will share a room; Ian protests furiously in private, but his own protective instincts make him give in. Jay starts a neighborhood gym with Rus and, on impulse, brings the whole team seasonal coffees — Ian quietly drinks the entire sweet maple-bourbon latte Jay picked for him.

### Chapter 15: Inferiority
Anxious about his modest credentials, Jay spends the Easter long weekend alone working through advanced economics texts. One evening after he leaves, Ian finds his annotated textbook and spends an hour reading Jay's humble, curious margin notes — one puzzling over Ian's own foresight — and finally admits to himself that his feelings have outgrown his control.

### Chapter 16: Preparations
Ian brings Jay along to a Calder Wetherby Trust visit and an expensive lunch where he opens up about his privileged upbringing — an unexpected intimacy that leaves Jay acutely, uneasily aware of him on the drive back. Bracing for the retreat, Jay endures a costly fashion-district expedition for resort wear and returns to Hathaway's for a quietly expensive evening outfit.

### Chapter 17: Retreat
The three-day beachfront retreat, where Jay and Ian share a suite, reshapes their relationship. Jay gets properly drunk at the sunset dinner under Ian's protective watch, conquers his first scuba dive at Ian's side, and the shared mornings, bonfires, and easy domesticity build an intimacy neither expected. Ian's puzzle-solving wins the team coveted concert tickets in the treasure hunt.

### Chapter 18: Artistic merits
The team descends on the experimental underground concert they won, where Leo turns up — having planted the prize himself. Bewildered by the punishing set, Jay and Ian escape to the bar and bond over their shared philistine bafflement; Ian reveals he played violin seriously through college before driving Jay home.

### Chapter 19: Family
Jay drives the company car home for Ema's graduation, where his designer clothes and luxury car make him a sensation and he accidentally overshadows her achievement. Back at the office, when his own attempts to photograph the place for Ema fail comically, Ian steps in to take proper shots — including a candid selfie Jay quietly treasures. Jay refuses to file for vacation unless Ian finally takes time off too.

### Chapter 20: Milestones
Jay turns thirty and reaches his goal of being completely debt-free, while across the city Ian agonizes over a birthday text he never sends. At his one-year review Ian re-offers the analyst role — Jay again declines — and gives him an extravagant watch, part anniversary gift and part apology for his cold first year. That night Ian finally admits to himself that he is hopelessly, impossibly in love with Jay, who shows no sign of seeing him as anything more than a mentor.

## Book 2 — Their second year

*From the morning after Ian privately falls in love to the night Jay finally
admits — to himself — that he loves Ian. Across the year their lives blend almost
all the way together: a foreign business trip, a near-collapse during strategy
season, a move to a home twelve minutes from Ian's, and a storm-bound company
retreat. By the end both are deeper in love than ever, and neither has said a
word. (Aug 2017 – Jul 2018. Condensed from `timeline/book2-timeline.md`.)*

### Chapter 21: Summer heat
The morning after privately admitting he loves Jay, Ian resolves to "manage" the feeling by ensuring Jay's happiness — and finds their dynamic quietly inverted, with Jay now fussing over his skipped meals and overwork. Both schedule the last week of October off; Jay books a solo tropical diving trip and starts lessons with Zoe, whose mentoring blossoms into the first real friendship of his city life. Over a sweltering apartment-hunting weekend Zoe comes out, and Jay, gently prompted, recognizes himself as content without romance.

### Chapter 22: Schemer and imp
Panicking about flying for the first time, Jay is quietly rescued when Ian invents an end-of-September business trip abroad as low-stakes "training." Leo, gleefully reading the real motive, both needles Ian into one feverish night of admitting his desire and warns Jay to be gentle with Ian's fragile heart — planting the first seed that Ian might feel something. Leo also books Jay into the city's most patronizing tailor, whose contempt reopens his class wound.

### Chapter 23: Fragility
Stung by the tailor, Jay retreats into polite distance, and Ian — unable to name the cause — fears he's done something to lose him. Luc's sudden week of "sick leave" turns out to be heartbreak: an eight-year relationship has ended, and Jay stays the night to comfort him, his first sleepover at thirty. With the tailoring finished and his diving course nearly complete, Jay steels himself for the trip.

### Chapter 24: Business trip
On the drive to the airport Ian reveals that he is a high-society pariah himself — even banned from the tailor that shamed Jay — recasting the whole humiliation. Jay's first flight and five-star stay go smoothly until a drunk executive calls him Ian's "beautiful escort"; Ian's fury is instant and he kills the deal, relenting only after the man is fired and the host makes amends with a lavish gift. A delayed, exhausted flight home ends with Ian quietly insisting that harassment is always the harasser's fault.

### Chapter 25: Tropical heat
Home to a studio overflowing with luxury he doesn't know how to absorb, Jay resets and hands out souvenirs, markedly more confident than the man who left. Q3 reporting then swallows everyone whole, and the busy season — which Ian had hoped would buy their cherished private evenings — instead keeps Jay constantly pulled away by the whole team. Afterward Jay flies solo to the tropics, earns his open-water certification, proves to himself that solo travel is not just survivable but his own, and returns to early-winter air.

### Chapter 26: Uncharted territories
Jay returns to find his postcard pinned to Ian's calendar; Ian, having spent his own lonely week finally accepting he is in love, resolves to simply love him "from the shadows." Honoring a half-forgotten promise to cheer up Luc, Jay organizes a chaotic group fencing night that — to his surprise — wins him real friends in Rus's gym crew. He gets serious about apartment-hunting, and Luc promptly guilt-trips him into a blind date.

### Chapter 27: Shared traumas
The blind date is a disaster — Jay is mocked and a "spilled" drink ruins his best suit — so he texts Ian, who rescues him within minutes and, worried, stays the night. The next morning, mortified to have made his refined boss sleep on a shabby couch, Jay listens as Ian reveals his own past: disowned with nothing, he sold his watch and rebuilt his whole life from a tiny studio over eight years. They trade their deepest wounds, Jay hugs him and insists none of it was his fault, and the trust between them deepens past anything before.

### Chapter 28: Unusually warm autumn
Jay invites himself into Ian's weekend — spreadsheets at his penthouse on Saturday, then a Sunday outing Jay plans himself: a ferry ride, a rustic bistro, a botanical garden, feeding ducks. For one day they move as equals, the happiest and most domestic time either has known. Which is exactly why returning to the glass wall on Monday leaves them both quietly bereft, each blaming the dying autumn weather.

### Chapter 29: Choices
Ian's agent shows Jay a flawless riverside flat — suspiciously cheap, and twelve minutes from Ian — and Jay, charmed yet certain it's "another scheme," can't bring himself to decide. Everyone he consults urges him to take it; Ian eventually half-confesses to nudging the agent, easing Jay's fear of a conspiracy without admitting his real motive. Decorating a private little tree in Ian's office, Jay resolves to choose after Christmas — and to spend the holiday with Ian rather than his prickly step-grandparents.

### Chapter 30: Winter lights
Christmas Day at Ian's — a tree, a cooked feast in matching aprons, sapphire cufflinks for Jay and an engraved lighter for Ian — is perfect and entirely unspoken. The quiet holiday week and a New Year's whiskey toast deepen the bubble before January's annual reporting and an obscene year-end bonus crash in. Rattled by a nightmare of Jay dying and forced to cancel their planned outing for a client emergency, Ian lets slip "You looked beautiful tonight" — and Jay, blushing, can't make sense of how much it shakes him.

### Chapter 31: Things that slip through fingers
A flooded, gloomy February and a brutal strategy season break Jay down: he accidentally drops a database table — the one error he was trained never to make — and, packing to move, throws out his emergency cash savings with the trash. Convinced he is fundamentally deficient, he cancels the apartment and begins fantasizing about retreating to a smaller, safer life. Ian, buried in the punishing workload his own decisions created, watches helplessly as Jay slowly disappears.

### Chapter 32: Alternatives
Jay quietly starts job-hunting, lands an interview at a modest firm, and is startled to find he has outgrown his old terror of such rooms — but the thought of never seeing Ian again undoes him. Fay names it plainly: he is head over heels in love. After a drunken night with the gym crew confirms the diagnosis he keeps denying, Jay decides he cannot leave; he will stay and endure, however much it costs.

### Chapter 33: What's worth fighting for
As the team literally lives in the office, Jay attends a second interview and, when an offer comes the very day strategy ships, turns it down to stay. Ian — misreading the smiling call as an acceptance and despairing — instead finds Jay still there on Monday, and at a long-overdue review apologizes for the season and offers a permanent contract, a steep raise, and a new "coordinator" title. Jay accepts, reverses the apartment cancellation, and the team is set to expand.

### Chapter 34: Damp weather
Spring brings relief: the workload eases, Q1 reports go more smoothly, and a four-times-normal paycheck lands. Jay signs the mortgage paperwork and finally visits his family for Easter, ferrying his siblings around in the company limousine while his mother, tearful, tells him she is proud. He lets himself plan the move at last — and, despite constant self-correction, keeps noticing how magnetic Ian is.

### Chapter 35: Home
Jay moves into the riverside flat with help from Rus's crew, his coworkers, Ian — and an uninvited, gift-bearing Leo making amends. A rowdy housewarming gives way to Ian quietly staying over, and the next blissful morning Jay hands him a spare key "in case of emergency." Settling into a home ten minutes from Ian, the new life feels almost too good to be real.

### Chapter 36: Foolish fantasies
A new senior analyst arrives — Ray Shore, brilliant and bluntly anti-social, whom Ian trusts precisely because he hides nothing — and Ian, secretly engineering himself more free evenings, has Jay help interview the junior hires. Trusted to pick the new staff, Jay feels both seen and quietly heartbroken, and his intrusive thoughts about Ian grow harder to suppress. As the company retreat nears, he packs without anxiety for once, dreading only his own feelings.

### Chapter 37: Thunderstorm
Storms confine the mountain retreat indoors, and Jay and Ian spend it inseparable — blending a shared signature scent, surviving an avant-garde fashion show, winning the cook-off on Ian's masterful cooking, and drinking far too much absinthe until Jay falls asleep in Ian's bed. They wake hungover but together, recover side by side at the spa, and the finance team wins a thermal-springs getaway as a prize. The closeness echoes Book 1's retreat — a world with the roles briefly stripped away.

### Chapter 38: New faces
The team fills out with charming senior analyst Liv (whom Ian distrusts on principle) and two juniors — one of whom, Gal, fixates on Jay with escalating, uninvited attention. The retreat's intimacy both bonds Ian and Jay and quietly rebuilds the walls between them at the office, where every shared glance goes unspoken. Ian, jealous and convinced he is losing Jay to a wider, brighter life, begins driving him home each night — the one closeness he lets himself keep.

### Chapter 39: Facing the truth
July's reporting crunch sharpens Gal's harassment until, at the summer quarterly party, a drunk Gal corners Jay and Ian extracts him to the terrace. Naming her conduct for what it is and shielding Jay again, Ian — standing close in the city lights — finally makes Jay admit to himself the truth he has been fleeing: he is hopelessly in love. The next day Ian arrives for Jay's birthday with a coffee maker chosen to say "I notice what you need" — gift and agony at once.

### Chapter 40: The storm before the calm
Jay's family visits and Leo throws him a lavish birthday party — partly a scheme to soften Ian toward his own approaching 40th — where Ian fastens new cufflinks onto Jay with an intimacy that leaves him breathless. Then Gal, fired after one last cornering, files a false assault complaint that surveillance footage instantly disproves; Leo reveals Ian once survived the very same ordeal. Resolved to stay at Ian's side whatever it costs, Jay accepts a long, wordless embrace at his door — both men deeper in love than ever, and both still saying nothing.
