# Book 1 — Chapter Summaries

*Clouds Over Gethsemane is a slowburn BL romance set in high-stakes finance — the
gradual, unspoken pull between **Ian Hagen**, a guarded 38-year-old CFO scarred by
an old betrayal, and **Jay Hall**, the 29-year-old assistant who joins his team
fleeing harassment. Book 1 covers Jay's first year at Turner & Associates, from his
desperate arrival to the night Ian privately admits he is in love.*

## Chapter 1: Things set in motion
Leo Turner pressures his best friend Ian into hiring Jay as his assistant, reopening the wound of Ian's betrayal by Sam Connelly, a corporate spy who once posed as his secretary. Jay, fleeing sexual harassment at his old job, is privately relieved to land under a boss famous for his coldness toward office romance. Jay's earnest competence only deepens Ian's suspicion — to him it reads as exactly what a skilled manipulator would project.

## Chapter 2: Fitting in
Jay's disciplined, isolated weekends and his exhausting effort to "perform normalcy" at team lunches establish who he is. When he makes a small error in his projections, Ian sits down and patiently walks him through the fix instead of scolding him, confusing Jay's sense of their dynamic. Drinking with Leo afterward, Ian refuses to soften — some lessons are too expensive to learn twice.

## Chapter 3: Establishing himself
Settling in, Jay earns Ian's rare praise by reframing an anxious client's emails during a strategy session. He survives his first Friday drinks and completes his first month, stunned to find his pay includes a large standard bonus — which he quietly banks, mindful that two months of probation remain.

## Chapter 4: The ripples
Ian shuts Jay down coldly when he asks about an old acquisition, and Jay retreats into deliberate invisibility. Max returns and loudly greets Jay as "Leo's joke hire"; that night Jay finds the old espionage scandal and the gossip about Ian's "type" online, and his safe harbor begins to feel false — though still safer than where he came from.

## Chapter 5: Finding his place
At his first lavish quarterly party Jay feels like an impostor, given only cold courtesy by Ian. Ian tries to signal Jay's worth by handing him a sensitive compliance audit, which Jay delivers superbly but mechanically. Max apologizes for the "joke hire" jab, and at his three-month review Jay accepts a permanent contract — feeling hollow, his security bought by staying invisible.

## Chapter 6: Regaining ground
Driving Jay home after the celebration, Leo admits appearance factored into the hire but insists Jay has earned his place on merit. Ian, reviewing a spotless background check, finally accepts that Jay is simply a wounded young man and resolves to become the fair mentor he needs. A late-night Fairfield crisis worked through together begins to thaw the ice between them.

## Chapter 7: A sense of security
Jay starts to read Ian's formal distance as professional integrity rather than rejection — the safe workplace he's always needed. At the Hartwell celebration dinner at The Palladium, Ian deftly shields him from Turner Senior's probing. Meanwhile Ian buries himself in punishing work to smother his growing feelings, and Jay quietly streamlines his load without being asked.

## Chapter 8: The festive season
December brings tree-decorating beside Ian, Jay's first personal decorations, and a custom tuxedo ordered for the gala; he ships gifts to his family and chooses to stay in the city for Christmas. Leo's remark about Ian's lonely, family-less holidays leaves Jay worried for him. On Christmas night Ian wakes from a vivid dream of kissing Jay and, rattled, drags Leo to dawn tennis and brutalizes him for hours — while Leo names the fear out loud: Ian wants Jay and is terrified of it.

## Chapter 9: New Year
Over the quiet skeleton-crew week between the holidays, Jay realizes how completely he trusts Ian, and Ian shares his best whiskey in a rare show of warmth. Jay's $80,000 year-end bonus — more than he has ever held at once — goes toward three tailored suits. He readies himself for the gala with an expensive haircut and culls the cheap interview suit he first arrived in.

## Chapter 10: The high finance
At the black-tie gala Jay looks striking and Ian, visibly affected, abandons networking to hover protectively. Separated from him, Jay is cornered and groped by a predatory client, Cruver; Ian finds him in tears, slips them out a back exit, and gently defuses the trauma over tea at Jay's apartment — secretly resolving to make Cruver pay. At his six-month review Jay shocks everyone by declining a promotion to analyst, choosing to stay as Ian's assistant — his true feelings showing in a choice he can't yet name.

## Chapter 11: February rush
Strategy season peaks and Jay becomes newly, physically aware of Ian in close quarters. Ian hands him a company credit card, and Jay uses it to lift the exhausted team's morale with an elaborate dinner that even draws Ian into the kitchen. HR's notices about the May retreat begin surfacing in the background — something Jay isn't ready to contemplate.

## Chapter 12: Stormy weather
A violent ice storm leaves Jay drenched and late for the first time; he buys a designer outfit from Hathaway's, not realizing how it unsettles Ian. Days later a severe flu fells him — bedridden and alone for over a week with his work phone dead — while Ian grows frantic at the silence. Jay returns pale and thin to a lavish "standard procedure" care package that warms him even as the illness leaves him freshly aware of his isolation.

## Chapter 13: Ides of March
Jay misfiles a legal notice and misses a compliance deadline; Ian corrects him gently, but Jay spirals until Luc takes him for spiked hot chocolate and shares his own rookie disaster. Meanwhile Ian's months-long investigation into Orrey Capital Partners — Cruver's employer — comes to fruition. Orrey is terminated amid a federal investigation, and Jay feels vindictive relief that he'll never face Cruver again.

## Chapter 14: Spring
April's first quarterly crunch — seven-day weeks, midnight finishes — finally teaches Jay why the bonuses are so generous. Leo engineers the May retreat's "mentor" rooming so that Ian and Jay will share a room; Ian protests furiously in private, but his own protective instincts make him give in. Jay starts a neighborhood gym with Rus and, on impulse, brings the whole team seasonal coffees — Ian quietly drinks the entire sweet maple-bourbon latte Jay picked for him.

## Chapter 15: Inferiority
Anxious about his modest credentials, Jay spends the Easter long weekend alone working through advanced economics texts. One evening after he leaves, Ian finds his annotated textbook and spends an hour reading Jay's humble, curious margin notes — one puzzling over Ian's own foresight — and finally admits to himself that his feelings have outgrown his control.

## Chapter 16: Preparations
Ian brings Jay along to a Calder Wetherby Trust visit and an expensive lunch where he opens up about his privileged upbringing — an unexpected intimacy that leaves Jay acutely, uneasily aware of him on the drive back. Bracing for the retreat, Jay endures a costly fashion-district expedition for resort wear and returns to Hathaway's for a quietly expensive evening outfit.

## Chapter 17: Retreat
The three-day beachfront retreat, where Jay and Ian share a suite, reshapes their relationship. Jay gets properly drunk at the sunset dinner under Ian's protective watch, conquers his first scuba dive at Ian's side, and the shared mornings, bonfires, and easy domesticity build an intimacy neither expected. Ian's puzzle-solving wins the team coveted concert tickets in the treasure hunt.

## Chapter 18: Artistic merits
The team descends on the experimental underground concert they won, where Leo turns up — having planted the prize himself. Bewildered by the punishing set, Jay and Ian escape to the bar and bond over their shared philistine bafflement; Ian reveals he played violin seriously through college before driving Jay home.

## Chapter 19: Family
Jay drives the company car home for Ema's graduation, where his designer clothes and luxury car make him a sensation and he accidentally overshadows her achievement. Back at the office, when his own attempts to photograph the place for Ema fail comically, Ian steps in to take proper shots — including a candid selfie Jay quietly treasures. Jay refuses to file for vacation unless Ian finally takes time off too.

## Chapter 20: Milestones
Jay turns thirty and reaches his goal of being completely debt-free, while across the city Ian agonizes over a birthday text he never sends. At his one-year review Ian re-offers the analyst role — Jay again declines — and gives him an extravagant watch, part anniversary gift and part apology for his cold first year. That night Ian finally admits to himself that he is hopelessly, impossibly in love with Jay, who shows no sign of seeing him as anything more than a mentor.
