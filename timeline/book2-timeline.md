# Book 2 Timeline

**Canonical years:** 2017–2018  
**Canonical start date:** Friday, August 4, 2017 (the morning after Book 1's close)  
**Chapters covered:** 21–40 (complete) — Arc 1 (Ch 21–25: the quiet acceptance → the New Continent trip → the solo diving vacation), Arc 2 (Ch 26–30: autumn social experiments → the apartment decision → Christmas and the year-end gala), Arc 3 (Ch 31–35: the February spiral → the new contract → the move), and Arc 4 (Ch 36–40: new hires → the storm-bound retreat → Gal's downfall → Jay's birthday).

> **Why 2017–2018 works — and where it stops working.** Book 2 opens "the next morning" after Book 1 ends (Ian's one-year review, dated ~Thu Aug 3, 2017), so it begins **Friday, August 4, 2017**. The autumn-and-winter spine locks onto the **real 2017–2018 calendar** with no fudging: the New Continent trip departs a Thursday at the end of September (**Thu Sep 28, 2017**), Halloween passes while Jay is away diving, **Christmas Day is a Monday (Dec 25, 2017)**, **New Year's Eve a Sunday (Dec 31)**, and the annual black-tie gala lands on a **Saturday (Jan 20, 2018)** — every one of these matches the actual day of week.
>
> From **spring 2018 onward the manuscript's weekday/holiday labels drift** off the real calendar (it places Easter in mid-April rather than its real April 1, and treats July 14 as a Sunday when it is a Saturday). Where that happens, dates below follow the manuscript's *internal* ordering and are marked **(est.)**. Soft/estimated dates are marked **(est.)** throughout. See **Timeline drift & flags** at the bottom — Book 2's loosest stretch is the **late-August/September social-and-tailor sequence**, which the prose narrates more densely than a strict weekday count allows.

## Timeline drift & flags

Places where the prose resists a clean calendar, ordered roughly by how much they'd affect an edit:

1. **The late-August/September sequence is over-packed (Ch 21–23).** Between the Aug 4 opening and the firm **Thu Sep 28** trip departure, the prose stacks: Ian's vacation planning, ~four diving lessons, three tailor fittings, Max/Luc's holiday, Luc's sick week, and Luc's breakup-sleepover — more "Monday" and "Saturday" scene-openers than a strict weekday count cleanly allows. Dates here are best-fit (est.), anchored on the trip and on the season labels ("end of August," "September started"). **Ch 21 and Ch 22 overlap by a few days** (~Aug 14–19): Ch22 S1's week runs alongside Ch21's final scenes, and Ch21 S5 (Jay/Zoe, Sat Aug 19) is parallel to Ch22 S2 (Leo visits Ian, same Saturday).

2. **Tailor-fitting spacing contradicts itself (Ch 22–23).** Ch22 S4 says return "next Saturday… then again the following week" (three *consecutive* Saturdays), but Ch22 S5 says the final fitting is "in two weeks." Dated here as **Sat Aug 26 / Sat Sep 2 / Sat Sep 16** (first two consecutive, the last two weeks apart) to satisfy both cues and leave room before the Sep 28 departure. The suits are then collected **Wed Sep 27**.

3. **"Month drawing to a close" vs. early October (Ch 25 S1–S2).** On return from the trip, the prose twice frames it as late September ("with the month drawing to a close"), but the hard anchors (Thu Sep 28 departure → Mon Oct 2 return, then Q3 reports "in October") place these scenes in **early October**. Treated as an authorial lag; the firm dates win.

4. **Diving-vacation dates vs. "last week of October" (Ch 25 S5).** Ian's leave was set for "the last week of October," but Jay's diving trip leaves a **Saturday**, has **Halloween (Oct 31) pass while he's away**, and returns a **Saturday to "November air."** Only a **Sat Oct 28 – Sat Nov 4** window satisfies all three, so the realized vacation straddles the month boundary rather than sitting inside October.

5. **Christmas-week ordering and gift timing (Ch 29–30).** The office tree-decorating (Ch29 S5), the Christmas party (Ch30 S1), the family gift-shopping, and the "last day before the holidays" all crowd the same ten days before **Mon Dec 25**. Tree (~Mon Dec 18) and party (~Fri Dec 22) are placed to keep the festive order sensible; Ch30 S2's "the next morning" (after the last workday) glosses over the intervening weekend to land on Christmas Day, and the family gifts must ship on a tight schedule to arrive Christmas morning.

6. **November/December labeling lag (Ch 28–29).** Ch28 S5 ("as November slowly ended") and Ch29 S4 ("November had ended") both reference November while the surrounding events compute to **early December**. The Ch28 weekend "date" (Sat–Sun Dec 2–3) and Ch29's apartment-viewing/laser-tag run press right up against each other; treated as overlapping rather than strictly sequential.

7. **Easter is placed in mid-April, not its real date (Ch 34).** The manuscript runs **Q1 reports first, then Easter, then the mid-April move**, which forces Easter to roughly **April 13–16, 2018**. The actual 2018 Easter was **April 1**. Book 1's timeline honored the real Easter (April 2017); Book 2 instead treats Easter as a mid-April, post-Q1 event. Dated per the manuscript's internal order and flagged.

8. **Mid-2018 weekday labels drift ~1 day (Ch 39–40).** From summer 2018 the prose's days of week run slightly ahead of the real calendar. **Jay's birthday** is narrated as a **Sunday** with the gym "the day before," but his canonical date, **July 14, 2018, is a Saturday**. Likewise the family-visit day off is called **"Friday the nineteenth,"** though **July 19, 2018 is a Thursday** (the Friday is the 20th). Dates here keep the canonical **July 14** birthday and the manuscript's Fri-party → Sat-gym → Sun-birthday sequence, with the offsets flagged.

9. **Ian's age and his September 40th (Ch 26, Ch 40).** Ian is called "thirty-nine" in November 2017 (Ch26), and Leo states he "turns forty in September" (Ch40) — i.e., **September 2018**, the opening of Book 3. This implies Ian turned 39 in September 2017, which is a hair off the series-bible "38 at series start (August 2016)" (he would have just turned 38 the prior September). Minor character-age wrinkle, not a date conflict; noted for the Book 3 birthday setup.

10. **Two Book 3 hooks are planted here.** The **finance team's thermal-springs prize** (Ch37 S5) and **Ian's 40th-birthday celebration** (seeded by Leo in Ch40 S5) are both set up at the end of Book 2 and pay off in Book 3 (the hot-springs retreat and Ian's 40th).

11. **Soft interior dating throughout 2018.** Once the manuscript stops giving hard weekday/holiday anchors (roughly February onward), most scene dates are reasoned from relative cues ("the following week," gym-Tuesdays, "as July approached") and the fixed quarterly/retreat/birthday tentpoles rather than from text-stated dates. All such values are marked **(est.)**.
