# Book 3 Timeline

**Canonical year:** 2018  
**Canonical start date:** Thursday, August 2, 2018 (Jay's two-year anniversary at Turner & Associates — the morning after Book 2's closing embrace)  
**Chapters covered:** 41–48 — **Book 3 is in progress; these are the first 8 chapters of an ongoing book.** Arc 1 (Ch 41–43: new traditions → the fortieth-birthday gala), Arc 2 (Ch 44–45: the afterglow → the hot-springs retreat), and Arc 3 (Ch 46–48: the island "fake couple" → the undertow home).

> **Why 2018 works — and it works cleanly here.** Book 3 opens "the next morning" after Book 2's final scene, on Jay's **two-year anniversary** (he started Mon Aug 1, 2016), and runs to the end of November 2018. Its hard anchors line up with the **real 2018 calendar** with almost no fudging: the **fortieth-birthday gala is a Saturday (Sep 22)**, the hot-springs weekend departs a **Friday (Oct 26, the last weekend of October)**, the island trip departs a **Monday (Nov 5)**, and Tia/Amy return the first **Monday of September (Sep 3)**. The ~1-day weekday drift that crept into Book 2's spring/summer has effectively resolved.
>
> Two soft spots remain (see **Timeline drift & flags**): the manuscript calls Jay's anniversary a **Thursday** (Aug 1, 2016 was a Monday, so two years lands on Wed Aug 1, 2018 — dated here as **Thu Aug 2**), and it calls the last day of September a **Monday** (Sep 30, 2018 is a Sunday). Soft/estimated dates are marked **(est.)** throughout. Most of the day-to-day rhythm — Tuesday runs, Friday dinners, Saturday gym — is reasoned from those weekly cues plus the firm tentpoles (the gala, the two trips), not from text-stated dates.

## Chapter 41: New traditions

### Scene 1: Companions
Start Date: Thursday, August 2, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Thursday  
Duration: 1 day  
Two years to the day since Jay arrived (and the morning after Ian's embrace closed Book 2). With Gal gone, the office breathes again. Over an after-work dinner — pointedly *not* a business dinner — Ian thanks Jay for staying and calls them "companions." Jay goes home and admits, fully, that he is completely in love.  
Milestone: Jay's two-year anniversary; the first "companions" dinner  
Note: Picks up directly from Book 2 Ch40 (the embrace was ~Wed Aug 1).

### Scene 2: Obvious favouritism
Start Date: Saturday, August 4, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Saturday  
Duration: 1 day  
A gym session with Rus (Jay finally tells him the full Gal story), then the dreaded tailor fitting (a birthday gift from Leo). That afternoon Leo barges in with champagne and a scheme: a magnificent fortieth-birthday party for Ian. Leo names what's obvious — how much Ian matters to Jay — and recruits him to "test the waters."  
Milestone: Leo proposes Ian's 40th-birthday party

### Scene 3: Vanilla and spices
Start Date: Monday, August 6, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Monday onward (~1 week)  
Duration: ~1 week  
Jay sounds Ian out; Ian reluctantly agrees to the party ("no party hats, no speeches"). Stuck on what to give a man who has everything, Jay decides on **homemade infusions/tinctures** in his grandfather's style, and turns his kitchen into a small distillery. **Tia Larson** is settled on as Gal's replacement. Slow, well-staffed summer (Ray and Liv keep overtime away).  
Milestone: Ian agrees to the party; Jay begins the tincture gift; Tia chosen

### Scene 4: Safe love
Start Date: Sunday, August 12, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Sunday–Monday  
Duration: 2 days  
Jay reasons himself into "safe love" — he'll want nothing, demand nothing, just keep showing up — even as intrusive thoughts about being touched by Ian intrude. Over a lounge coffee, Ian gently corrects Jay's memory of his early days: he was never a "drama" like Gal, just frightened and trying to disappear, which "broke my heart."

### Scene 5: Running
Start Date: Tuesday, August 14, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Tuesday onward  
Duration: ~1 week  
A new tradition: **Tuesday evening runs** along the river with Ian (replacing Jay's gym slot). Jay struggles; Ian coaches patiently. Swimming, exercise, and another **Friday dinner** thread their lives tighter together.  
Milestone: The Tuesday-run tradition begins

## Chapter 43: The celebration

### Scene 1: Gold lions and carved dragons
Start Date: Monday, September 10, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Monday onward (~1.5 weeks)  
Duration: ~1.5 weeks  
Jay secures Eli's invitation from Leo and recruits Ian's contacts to help find Tim's car. Leo, snooping in Jay's tincture-filled kitchen, teases that Jay clearly adores Ian. The final week before the gala closes in; Jay chooses his three best tinctures and boxes them with a handwritten note.

### Scene 2: Incandescent
Start Date: Tuesday, September 18, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Tuesday–Saturday  
Duration: ~5 days  
Rain cancels Tuesday's run. Leo arranges a full Cinderella treatment for the girls (car, dresses, hair) and buries Jay in pointless day-of "errands" as deliberate sabotage. Friday the girls arrive; Saturday Jay is styled, then made over an hour late by the errands. He descends the gala staircase and the room turns — **Ian forgets the entire party at the sight of him.**  
Milestone: Jay's grand entrance; "incandescent"

### Scene 3: Ian's world
Start Date: Saturday, September 22, 2018  
Day: Saturday (evening)  
Duration: same night  
The "hundred-person" party is **five hundred** — politicians, old money, artists, and an unapologetically queer crowd: Ian's actual, glittering world. Jay, terrified but resolved to "live up to it," spends the night orbiting Ian, guarding Ema and Lam (Rus and Van on sentry duty), and shepherding the team.  
Milestone: Ian's fortieth-birthday gala

### Scene 4: Not boyfriend
Start Date: Saturday, September 22, 2018  
Day: Saturday (night)  
Duration: same night  
Fireworks, a four-tier cake, a vaudeville star, a full orchestra. One of Ian's old friends mistakes Jay for Ian's "boyfriend"; both deny it, and Jay deflects every advance with a firm no. Ian aches to claim him and can't. Jay introduces Eli to Ian; at 2 a.m. he takes the exhausted, exhilarated girls home.  
Milestone: The "boyfriend" mistake; Jay deflects all advances

### Scene 5: You deserve every good thing
Start Date: Sunday, September 23, 2018  
Day: Sunday  
Duration: 1 day  
Home at 4 a.m., Ian ignores the mountain of gifts and goes straight to Jay's — three crystal bottles and a note ("You deserve every good thing"). He tastes the red-currant tincture and thinks *it probably tastes like his lips.* Leo visits to needle him; Ian, territorial, won't share a drop. Meanwhile Jay frets that he turned Ian's birthday into an exhausting spectacle.  
Milestone: Ian opens Jay's gift — the emotional heart of the gala

## Chapter 44: Afterglow

### Scene 1: Early darkness

Day: Monday onward  
Duration: ~1 week  
Jay apologizes for the gala's excess; Ian absolves him and admits his living room is now "a warehouse" of unwanted gifts. Jay offers to help sort them Saturday. The **Tuesday runs are paused until spring** (autumn dark and cold, reporting season looming).

### Scene 2: Thoughtful gifts

An afternoon sorting gifts on Ian's floor — domestic and intimate. Standouts: a two-person **sauna voucher** (from Rus and the gym crew), a "matching" gym hoodie, **botanical-garden night tickets** from Ema and Lam (a spring date Ian is openly delighted by), and Leo's absurd gem-encrusted **brooch** (which Jay laughs himself sick over). Leo announces the **hot-springs prize trip for the last weekend of October**. A Friday regulatory emergency costs them a Friday dinner.  
Milestone: Q4 reports (effortless); island trip booked; hot-springs date set

Start Date: Saturday, October 13, 2018 (est.)

Day: Friday–Sunday  
Duration: 3 days  
Ian pines at home over Jay's tincture. Jay arrives to a warm family welcome; **Saturday is Tim's eighteenth birthday**, and the car reveal overwhelms everyone (even Jay's stepfather thaws). Ema admits Leo steered the botanical-garden gift. Jay trains home Sunday, leaving the car with Tim.  
Milestone: Tim's 18th birthday; the car gift

## Chapter 45: Steam and stars

### Scene 1: Salt-ocean smell
Start Date: Monday, October 22, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Monday onward  
Duration: ~1 week  
Back at work, the team clears the decks before the trip. Jay shops for winter gear (a ridiculous earflap hat). **Friday morning** they fly to **Ashland** (two hours north); even **Ray comes** ("I love hot springs"). At cliff-side **Frostbay Sanctuary**, rooms are paired — Ian with Jay.  
Milestone: The hot-springs prize trip begins (Fri, Oct 26)

### Scene 2: Aurora
Start Date: Friday, October 26, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Friday (night)  
Duration: same night  
After hours soaking, a freezing walk, and a hearty floor-cushion dinner, the **aurora** appears over an ocean-edge hot pool — the most beautiful thing Jay has ever seen. Back in the suite, wine and heat put Jay to sleep mid-sentence; Ian covers him and realizes he may never have been this happy.  
Milestone: The aurora night

### Scene 3: Very interesting wood
Start Date: Saturday, October 27, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Saturday  
Duration: 1 day  
A morning hike goes wrong — Jay, in only a sweater (no thermal base layers), nearly freezes and Ian marches him back. The afternoon recovers on a heated terrace. Then a **communal northern-style (nude) sauna** — Ray's unprecedented enthusiasm makes it unmissable — leaves Jay staring fixedly at "very interesting wood," helplessly aware of Ian beside him.  
Milestone: The communal sauna

### Scene 4: Pitiful plea
Start Date: Saturday, October 27, 2018  
Day: Saturday (night)  
Duration: same night  
Rattled by the sauna, Jay over-drinks at dinner; Ian walks him out into the cold. Clinging to Ian's warmth, Jay whispers **"I want you to hold me for the rest of my life… just tonight, please."** Ian holds him to sleep and, into the dark, answers what Jay won't remember: **"I would. God help me, I would."**  
Milestone: Jay's drunken plea; Ian's whispered answer — the emotional peak of the retreat

### Scene 5: Cruel joke
Start Date: Sunday, October 28, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Sunday  
Duration: 1 day  
Jay wakes mortified and half-remembering; Ian, hollowed out, insists "nothing happened" and blames a migraine. They massage, soak, and fly home in fraught quiet. Alone at last, Ian lets himself fall apart — Jay said exactly what Ian most wanted to hear, and that is the cruellest part.  
Milestone: Return from the hot springs

## Chapter 46: Paradise found

### Scene 1: Dispelling doubts

Day: Monday onward (~1 week)  
Duration: ~1 week  
A punishing pre-vacation week of handovers (Ian setting a relentless pace, possibly to avoid thinking). Over a Saturday lunch he tells Jay plainly to stop treating their friendship "as something you need to earn back," then they shop for beach clothes and factor-fifty sunscreen.  
Milestone: Ian dispels the post-retreat tension

### Scene 2: Encounters
Start Date: Monday, November 5, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Monday  
Duration: 1 day  
They fly to the **Songbird Islands** — a palace-like cliff resort, a two-bedroom suite over the sea, **ten days**. At the bar a woman flirts with Jay; Ian smoothly intercepts (inventing dinner reservations). The first of many rescues, and the first of the trip's running themes: Jay is the most beautiful person in every room and the only one who doesn't know it.  
Milestone: The Songbird Islands vacation begins (Mon, Nov 5)

### Scene 3: Nuisance
Start Date: Tuesday, November 6, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Tuesday  
Duration: 1 day  
Slow mornings with coffee on the terrace; Ian swims punishing laps while Jay floats. A pushy man corners Jay by the pool; he extracts himself, and Ian gently reframes it — Jay's warmth isn't a flaw, the harassment isn't his fault. Jay sulks pointedly while Ian does an hour of email.

### Scene 4: Past relationships
Start Date: Tuesday, November 6, 2018  
Day: Tuesday (evening)  
Duration: same night  
At the resort's most romantic restaurant, the two trade their full romantic histories: **Ian's** (out to himself at fifteen, a conditional father, a volatile fling, a year-long relationship that failed on his unavailability, a managed-distraction phase, then Sam, then seven years alone) and **Jay's** (a one-night non-event, a two-month relationship that ended with "not interesting enough," the first-job harassment, then nothing).  
Milestone: The mutual confession of pasts

### Scene 5: Breaking point
Start Date: Thursday, November 8, 2018 (est.)  
Day: Thursday  
Duration: 1 day  
The fourth evening. The persistent man returns; Ian declares "Jay is not available," then calls Jay **beautiful** (the second time ever) — and Jay short-circuits. Worn down by a week of Jay's nearness he can't claim, Ian drinks himself into a silent, total shutdown. **Jay rescues him for once**, putting him to bed and tending his hangover the next day. Ian, disarmed, promises the sunset cruise.  
Milestone: Ian's breaking point; the roles reverse

Day: Monday

## Timeline drift & flags

Book 3 is the cleanest-dating book so far; the calendar mostly cooperates. Ordered by how much each would affect an edit:

1. **Two-year anniversary: date vs. weekday (Ch 41).** Ch41 opens "Thursday… exactly two years since he'd walked into Turner & Associates." Jay's first day was **Monday, Aug 1, 2016**, so two years lands on **Wed, Aug 1, 2018** — but the manuscript says Thursday, so it's dated **Thu, Aug 2** here. (Book 1's one-year review was likewise framed as a Thursday in early August.) Loose, not a real conflict.

2. **"Last day of September" called a Monday (Ch 44 S3).** The Q4 reporting run opens on the day after "the last day of September… Monday." **Sep 30, 2018 is a Sunday** (Oct 1 is the Monday). A one-day slip — a faint echo of Book 2's summer drift. The reports begin in early October regardless; dated ~Oct 1–2.

3. **Island-trip length and return day are soft (Ch 46–47).** The trip is fixed at the front — **departs Mon, Nov 5** — and stated as "ten days," but the back end is reasoned scene-by-scene (arrival, pool/beach days, the fourth-evening collapse, the two-day cruise, a storm day, a museum day, a "doing nothing" day, the last day). That lands the return ~**Nov 15–16**, with the first office day the following **Monday (~Nov 19)**. Adjust if the intended return is a specific weekday.

4. **The gala may or may not fall on Ian's exact birthday (Ch 43).** Ian's birthday is established only as "**September**" (Book 2, Ch40). The fortieth-birthday gala is dated **Sat, Sep 22** from the run-up cues (Tia's Sep 3 start → "two weeks to the party"); whether that is his literal birthday or merely the celebration nearest it is never stated. Treat Sep 22 as the celebration date.

5. **Two new traditions and two forward hooks to track.** New recurring beats begin here — **Tuesday runs** (Ch 41, paused for winter in Ch 44, revived as home-gym sessions in Ch 48) and weekly **Friday dinners** (interrupted repeatedly, restored each time). Two future events are planted for spring 2019: the **botanical-garden night date** (Ema/Lam's gift) and a **Brussels/Geneva work trip with an April conference**. Christmas 2018 is floated at the very end as "still time" — the likely next beat.

6. **Book 3 is unfinished.** These 8 chapters (41–48) cover **Aug–late Nov 2018** and end mid-scene in the relationship's arc (desire finally undeniable on both sides, still unspoken). Dates, milestones, and the summary will extend as further chapters land; nothing here closes the book.

7. **Soft interior dating throughout.** Outside the firm tentpoles (anniversary, gala, hot springs, island), most scene dates are reasoned from the weekly rhythm — Saturday gym, Tuesday runs, Friday dinners, "first week of September," "last weekend of October" — rather than text-stated dates, and are marked **(est.)**.
