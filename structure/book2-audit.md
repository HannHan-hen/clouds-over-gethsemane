# Book 2 — Structural Audit: Character Fidelity, Event Plausibility, Weak Points

A full read-through interrogation of all 20 chapters (21–40), checked against
`characters.md`, `guidelines/book2-craft-notes.md`, and — per the brief — **Book 1**,
to catch places where the two books have drifted out of sync.

**Scope / what's excluded.** Per instruction, the **calendar/weekday drift** already
logged in `timeline/book2-timeline.md` (the Easter-placement and July-14-weekday flags,
the over-packed Aug–Sep sequence, etc.) is **not** relitigated here. The Book 2 craft
notes are unusually thorough and already own most of the *intended* design — especially
the **faults** theme (Ian's controlling/vindictive streak, Jay's passivity), the
**held-confession** engine, and the acknowledged weakness of the **Gal** arc. Those are
**cross-referenced, not re-argued.** The value-add here is (a) concrete continuity/character
errors not captured anywhere, (b) a plausibility pass on the events, and (c) the
**cross-book reconciliation** the brief asked for.

Severity: **[HIGH]** reader will snag / breaks canon · **[MED]** fix on the revision pass ·
**[LOW]** polish.

## 2. Character fidelity

The leads stay **true to themselves and to the craft-notes intent.** Jay's recognition
arc (admiration → "definitely not love" → "hopelessly in love," ch39 terrace) is drawn
with the same care Book 1 gave Ian, and the mirror holds. Ian's shift from *suppressing*
the feeling to *engineering around it* is consistent and is, by design, a darker register.
Supporting cast keep their Book 1 voices (Leo the meddler-turned-truth-teller, Max warm and
tactless, Luc the social engine, Amy the anchor, Zoe quietly competent, Rus blunt and
protective). New hires land cleanly against `characters.md` (Ray the brilliant misanthrope,
Liv the suspiciously-charming senior, Rin the sweet clinger, Gal the dark-mirror antagonist).
The problems are **factual/continuity**, not characterization.

### 2.1 Jay's mother — surname collision **[MED]**

In **ch40 S2**, Leo greets her as **"Mrs. Hall"** ("You must be Mrs. Hall. I'm Leo
Turner."). One scene later, **ch40 S3**, Ian greets her as **"Mrs. Carter"** ("Mrs.
Carter. It's a pleasure to meet you properly."). `characters.md` is clear: she is **Ira
Carter (née Hall)** — Hall is her maiden name and *Jay's* surname; she took *Carter* on
marrying Ted. So **Leo's "Mrs. Hall" is the error** (Ian's "Mrs. Carter" is correct). Fix
Leo's line. (Minor knock-on: this is the only place the mother is addressed by surname in
the book, so it's the one spot a reader could be confused about who "Carter" even is.)

### 2.2 "Sandra" — a panelist who doesn't exist **[MED]**

In the Accord Financial second interview (**ch33 S2**), the panel is introduced as **Dan
Mercer, Liu Serrano, and Mos Parker** (with Meg the earlier phone contact). Mid-scene,
though: *"'Have you been in the industry long?' **Sandra** asked."* No Sandra is ever
introduced; the woman on the panel is **Liu Serrano**. "Sandra" is a leftover from an
earlier draft — reassign the line to Liu (or one of the three named panelists).

### 2.3 Walk-on registry gaps **[LOW]**

`characters.md`'s named-walk-on registry is meant to prevent name reuse. Several Book 2
named figures are missing from it (and one recurring confidante deserves a full entry, not
a walk-on line):

- **Fay** — the Café Lemon barista. Named here and a **recurring confidante across both
  books** (she's the unnamed barista in Book 1 ch5; named and load-bearing throughout Book 2
  — she's the one who first names Jay's love in ch32). She is **not in `characters.md` at
  all** and should have a proper character entry.
- **Yan** — the preening blind-date guest who mocks Jay and "accidentally" spills wine
  (ch27). Named antagonist of that arc; not logged.
- **Gav** — Luc's flatmate; recurs across ch26, ch27, ch28, ch40. Currently only *mentioned*
  inside Roc's registry line ("alongside Gav and Lim"); has no entry of his own.
- **May** — Ian's housekeeper (ch28, ch35). Recurring, named; not logged.
- **Jen** — "Jen from accounting," the recurring minor love-interest-foil (Book 1 ch5/ch17,
  Book 2 ch34). Still missing (carried over from the Book 1 audit).

Also: the existing **Ina** registry line says she's the "diving instructor on the **Songbird
Islands** trip," but in-text Ina teaches Jay's **local, in-city** pool lessons (ch21–25);
the tropical solo trip is to **Lagoon Belt** with instructor **Ash**. The "Songbird Islands"
attribution is wrong (Songbird Islands is a Book 3 locale). Tidy the note.

### 2.4 What's consistent and well-tracked (keep)

- **The watch/poverty echo lands without contradiction:** Ian *sold his watch* when disowned
  (ch27); Ian *gives Jay a watch* (Book 1 ch20). Jay treasures it; the thematic rhyme is
  clean.
- **The Book 1 retreat painting** (ch17) on Ian's wall (ch28) and the **new** painting "from
  the same artist" Ian gives Jay (ch35) are consistent.
- **Jay's "ace"/college-failure** setup (ch21) is deliberately *not* reconciled with falling
  for a man (ch39) — craft notes track this as a Book 2→3 thread, not an oversight.
- **Floor number is consistently the 34th** throughout Book 2 (ch26, ch40) — see §4 for why
  that matters against Book 1.

## 3. Event plausibility ("fictional but plausible")

The spine — two people building a shared life through reciprocal care while neither names it
— is sound. The strain is concentrated in the same few **wish-fulfillment / disproportion**
levers as Book 1, now one turn deeper. Note up front: the craft notes **deliberately** frame
Ian's covert arranging and his vindictive streak as *faults to establish honestly*, not as
near-misses to excuse. So several items below are "intended fault, with a plausibility wobble
in the **specifics**" rather than "this is wrong."

### 3.1 The apartment / mortgage / bank engineering **[MED — biggest stretch]**

Ian secretly **bribes the agent** to settle Jay 12 minutes from his door (ch29), then
arranges a flat at **roughly half market price** (ch29), a mortgage at **2% over 30 years,
no early-repayment penalty** at a bank that gives it because **"Mr. Hagen vouched for you
personally"** (ch30/ch35), plus a **comped parking spot and storage unit** slipped into the
contract (ch35). Craft notes correctly call the *control* a fault and defer the *reckoning*
to Book 3/4 — that's a design choice, not a bug.

The **plausibility** wobble is in the financial mechanics, not the ethics: a 2%/30-yr
no-penalty mortgage (presumably well under market) granted on a personal vouch, plus a flat
at half price, reads less like "good connections" and more like **Ian quietly subsidizing
Jay** — which is a different, larger thing than the text admits, and which a financially
careful character like Jay would be expected to price out and balk at harder than he does
("suspiciously cheap," then he signs). Recommend either dialing the terms toward *plausibly*
favorable (a market-beating-but-real rate; a genuine motivated-seller discount) **or** letting
Jay explicitly clock that the numbers don't add up to anything but a subsidy — which would
also *strengthen* the fault the craft notes want on the page.

### 3.2 The Gal false-accusation arc **[MED — weakest event chain]**

Already named in the craft notes as "the most forced thing in the book," so this is
reinforcement, not a new claim. From a plausibility/structure angle the specific snags are:

- **The resolution is frictionless to the point of convenience:** every room (including the
  archive room) has **audio + video** surveillance; the footage exonerates Jay **within ten
  minutes**; Gal is fired on the spot and the complaint is "removed from any records" (ch40).
  A serious, real-world-loaded event (a false assault claim) is raised and fully dissolved
  inside a single scene.
- **Weight/payoff mismatch:** that's a large trauma to spend and then drop. Jay "resolves to
  stay" and the book ends; the accusation is never referenced again. **Flag for Book 3:** the
  craft notes already ask that this not evaporate — honor it (aftershocks on Jay's trust, on
  how he reads new hires, on the office). It's the clearest unredeemed thread leaving Book 2.

### 3.3 Compensation — the escalation continues **[MED — keep aware]**

Book 1 flagged the comp as a wish-fulfillment lever; Book 2 pushes it further: a year-end
bonus **"more than double his entire previous year's income"** (ch30), an **80,000 / "four
times normal"** paycheck (ch34), and a **45% raise to 20k/month** with executive benefits
and a coordinator title (ch33) — for an EA-turned-coordinator. The year-end bonus *exceeding
2× annual income* is the most extreme single figure in either book. Two consequences worth a
conscious decision: (a) it's fine as deliberate fantasy, but it's now **large enough to
undercut Jay's persistent money-anxiety** (he agonizes over a flat he could nearly buy in
cash); (b) it retroactively **breaks Book 1's "you're doing analyst work for assistant pay"**
framing — by Book 2 Jay out-earns the framing entirely. Pick a lane on how poor Jay is
*allowed to still feel*.

### 3.4 Ian's disproportion as a recurring beat **[LOW — intended, just track it]**

The New Continent deal (ch24): Ian **terminates a major international partnership on the
spot** because a drunk exec calls Jay an "escort," then Mac fires Zac overnight and salvages
it with a **prototype-laptop gift** to Jay. This rhymes with Book 1's Cruver/Orrey
demolition — and the craft notes explicitly name Ian's *vindictive/over-protective streak*
as an intended fault, so it's in-character. The plausibility nits are the **conveniences**
(an executive fired overnight; a priceless prototype produced as an apology). Minor; note
only that "Ian nukes something disproportionately to protect Jay, and the world tidily
rewards it" has now happened twice — vary the *aftermath* so it doesn't become a fixed
mechanism.

### 3.5 Beat-level recurrence (cross-ref craft notes) **[LOW]**

- The **"both pine in parallel, twelve minutes / a wall apart" chapter coda** is genuinely
  near-identical across most back-half chapters (ch28, ch31, ch35, ch36, ch38, ch39, ch40 all
  close on it). Craft notes already mark this as the single clearest line-level target — keep
  3–4 strongest, vary/cut the rest.
- The **bonus-shock "stared at the number, refreshed it, it didn't change"** beat recurs
  (ch30, ch34) — craft-flagged; compress.
- The **retreat (ch37)** is again the most noticeable intimacy-jump of the book (absinthe →
  waking in Ian's bed), exactly as Book 1 ch17 was — an intended arc hinge, just the spot
  most against "grew close without noticing when."

## 4. Cross-book reconciliation (the brief: "we might have started mixing things up")

### 4.1 The half-siblings — RESOLVED, and it **corrects the Book 1 audit** **[HIGH]**

Book 2 gives the data Book 1 lacked, and the picture is cleaner than the Book 1 audit
concluded. **The manuscript is actually self-consistent; `characters.md` is the outlier.**

What the prose says, across both books:
- **B1 ch7 S2:** the *oldest* half-sibling is a boy (stepbrother born when Jay was ~12); the
  *youngest* is a boy "sixteen years younger than Jay"; Ema is the girl between.
- **B1 ch8 / ch15:** **Tim** is "the oldest" (multitool/camping; college applications); **Ari**
  is the youngest (building blocks).
- **B2 ch25/ch34:** **Ari is the youngest brother, age 13**, wants the hand-me-down laptop;
  **Tim** gets the premium watch and behaves as the elder.
- **B2 ch36:** the mother says **"your brother [Tim] is turning eighteen in autumn"** (autumn
  2018 = Book 3).

So **consistently: Tim = oldest, Ema = middle, Ari = youngest.** The Book 1 audit treated
this as a *manuscript* contradiction; it isn't. The B1 ch19 phrase "the younger brother"
means **younger than Jay** (everyone is), **not** "youngest sibling" — that was a misread.

The **real** errors are two, and both are now precise:
1. **`characters.md` mislabels Tim as "Youngest of Jay's half-siblings."** He is the
   **oldest**. (It's also internally self-contradictory: it calls Tim "late teens / turns 18"
   *and* "youngest," while Ari is 13.) → **Fix the Tim entry to "oldest."**
2. **Tim's age doesn't support "turns 18 in autumn 2018."** B1 ch8 (Dec 2016) calls Tim
   **"the oldest at seventeen"**; 17 in Dec 2016 means he'd turn 18 in **2017**, and be ~19
   by autumn 2018 — but B2 ch36 + the Book 3 car-for-his-18th plot need him **turning 18 in
   autumn 2018**. → **Lower Tim's Book 1 age** (B1 ch8 "seventeen" → ~"fifteen") so the
   load-bearing Book 3 beat works.

Recommended canonical decision (a one-line sibling bible):
> **Tim** (oldest, turns 18 autumn 2018 → ~15 in autumn 2016) · **Ema** (~15–16 in B1,
> graduating middle school; mid-teens) · **Ari** (youngest, ~12 in B1 / 13 in B2; math-gifted).

Then reconcile to it: `characters.md` (Tim "oldest," ages), **B1 ch8** ("seventeen" → lower),
and double-check Ema's "13–14" in `characters.md` against the text's 15.

> **Action for the Book 1 audit:** finding #1 there is now amended (see the note appended to
> `book1-audit.md`) — the manuscript isn't self-contradictory on birth order; the fix lives in
> `characters.md` + Tim's age.

### 4.2 Office floor: Book 2 confirms which Book 1 value is the typo **[LOW]**

Book 1 flagged a 32nd-vs-34th-floor inconsistency. **Book 2 uses the 34th floor
consistently** (ch26, ch40 — Jay even tells his family "Thirty-fourth floor"). That settles
it: **34th is canonical**, and Book 1's "thirty-second" (ch1 S1, ch3 S1) is the outlier to
correct.

### 4.3 Jay's employment history: Book 2 corroborates the Book 1 fix **[LOW]**

Book 1 flagged ch1's "three years at a … firm" (singular) against ch2's "six years at three
firms." Book 2 **backs the multi-job version**: ch27 details the *first-job* assault (married
boss → HR dismissal → quit → borrowed from his mother), and ch31 adds the *gas-station
weekend job* he worked to repay her and build the $10k envelope — i.e. a multi-employer
history with harassment at more than one. This reinforces that **B1 ch1's single-firm line is
the outlier** to widen.

### 4.4 Continuity that holds across the seam (good)

- Ian's **September birthday / age** is handled exactly as the timeline intends (he's 39 in
  Book 2; "thirty-nine-year-old," ch26) — no conflict.
- The **$10k cash envelope** (Book 1 ch11, characters.md) is the same one Jay loses in B2
  ch31. Consistent.
- **Cruver/Orrey** stays in the past where Book 1 left it (no contradictory callback).
