# Planting queue — small things to seed/reinforce during the editing pass

A living checklist for **forward setups** that have to be planted (or lightly reinforced) in
*already-written* chapters when we do the editing pass — the things easy to forget because the
payoff is books away. Keep entries **light and non-load-bearing**: these are seeds, not plot
machinery. Tick them off as the edit pass reaches the relevant chapters.

This is the place to look before editing any Book 1–3 chapter: "does anything need planting here?"

| Status | Plant | Where | For | Touch |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ☐ | **Jay's journaling habit** | ~3 light touches across **Books 1–3** | Book 4 journal scene (`guidelines/book4-craft-notes.md`) | very light |

## Details

### Jay's journaling habit → Book 4 journal scene

**Why:** Book 4 has a fully-planned scene where Jay gives Ian his journals to read (see
`guidelines/book4-craft-notes.md`, "The journal scene"). For "Ian sees Jay write one day" to land,
the reader must already know Jay keeps personal journals. Today the manuscript only shows *work*
handwriting/notes — no personal-journal habit.

**Plan:** plant it **lightly, from Book 1 onward — about three touches total across Books 1–3**, and
no heavier. There's a lot of room; it must stay background texture, never a defining trait.

**Candidate touches (pick ~3, spread out; exact spots chosen during the edit):**
- A **stack of journals** glimpsed on a shelf in Jay's studio — a one-line set-dressing detail
  (his apartment is described several times; e.g. B1 ch06, B2 ch22/ch36).
- Jay **writing one evening** and being quietly private about it — half a beat, not a scene.
- A **passing line** acknowledging the habit (e.g., tucked beside the existing "my idea of leisure
  is reading at home," B2 ch26) — keep it offhand.

**Cohere with the existing through-line** (the interior, self-documenting Jay): beautiful handwriting
(B1 ch01/ch11/ch15), the post-it "trademark" (B1 ch11), reading as leisure (B2 ch25/ch26/ch35). The
journals are the private cousin of all that — and they pay off Ian's B1 ch15 "reading Jay's margin
notes felt like private correspondence" beat. **Restraint is the whole point:** the through-line
works because it was planted lightly and never underlined.

**Done when:** ~3 light journal references exist across Books 1–3, none load-bearing.
