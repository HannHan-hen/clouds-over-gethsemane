# Planting queue — things to seed/reinforce during the editing pass

A living checklist for **forward setups and on-page reinforcements** that should be planted (or
lightly strengthened) in *already-written* chapters when we do the editing pass — the things easy to
forget because the payoff is chapters or books away, or because they make an existing intent
*legible* without changing the story.

Keep entries **light and in-register**: most of these are seeds, single lines, or a beat — not plot
machinery, and never at the cost of the quiet slice-of-life tone. **Check this file before editing
any Book 1–3 chapter:** "does anything need planting or reinforcing here?"

For the *reasoning* behind each item, follow the cross-reference. This queue is the **do-it** list;
the audits are the **why**.

> Scope note: this queue is for *plantings and reinforcements*. Pure **continuity-error fixes**
> (e.g. Tim's age, the `characters.md` sibling labels, the stray "Sandra", registry gaps) are
> tracked as findings in `book1-audit.md` / `book2-audit.md`, not here.

## Details

### ☐ Jay's journaling habit → Book 4 journal scene
*Ref: `guidelines/book4-craft-notes.md` "The journal scene."*

Book 4 has a fully-planned scene where Jay gives Ian his journals to read. For "Ian sees Jay write
one day" to land, the reader must already know Jay journals — today the manuscript only shows *work*
handwriting/notes. Plant it **lightly, from Book 1 onward — ~3 touches total across Books 1–3**, no
heavier; it must stay background texture.

Candidate touches (pick ~3, spread out): a **stack of journals** glimpsed on a shelf in Jay's studio
(his place is described at B1 ch06, B2 ch22/ch36); Jay **writing one evening**, quietly private about
it; a **passing line** beside the existing "my idea of leisure is reading at home" (B2 ch26). Coheres
with the interior-Jay through-line (beautiful hand B1 ch01/ch11/ch15; post-it "trademark" ch11;
reading B2 ch25/ch26/ch35) and pays off Ian's B1 ch15 "private correspondence" beat. **Restraint is
the point.** *Done when:* ~3 light references exist across Books 1–3, none load-bearing.

### ☐ Cruver "chilling seed" — Ian isn't finished
*Ref: `company-audit.md` §6 (the Cruver redesign).*

The redesign moves Ian's disproportionate, vindictive campaign against Cruver out of Book 1 (where a
three-month federal-case takedown is a thriller bolt-on) and into Book 2, as a slow reveal in the
"faults" register. Book 1 keeps the gala harassment and Ian's *proportionate* visible response — he
**fires Orrey as a client** (the Turner-Senior risk memo already does this, B1 ch13). The editing-pass
plant is small: after that, add **one chilling seed** that Ian isn't done — a couple of cold private
calls, or a single flat, frightening line the *reader* clocks and Jay doesn't. That seed is the whole
Book 1 gap-filler. *(The Book 2 campaign reveal is new material, not an edit — tracked in the craft
notes, not here.)* *Done when:* Book 1 plants the seed without spelling out the campaign.

### ☐ Discretionary-bonus relabel
*Ref: `company-audit.md` §4.*

**Keep** the opaque, surprising pay structure and every money beat (first-pay shock, the year-end
windfall, the raise, Jay's ~3-month bewilderment) — the opacity is a deliberate device for Jay's
money arc. The only change is **nomenclature**: wherever comp is described as a *guaranteed* monthly/
quarterly **bonus**, relabel to **discretionary** language — *profit-share*, *distribution*,
*allocation*, a *13th/14th-month custom*. That single swap reads as deliberate firm eccentricity
rather than an author who doesn't know finance. Keep the New-Year timing fingerprint (Ian personally
orders the year-end payout). *Done when:* no comp reference implies a contractually *guaranteed*
sub-annual bonus.

### ☐ Crown-jewel tier line
*Ref: `company-audit.md` §3/§7; `finance-primer.md` §3.*

One line, early, establishing that the **<20 marquee accounts are the elite tier**, while the "three
smaller teams" handle a longer tail of ordinary clients. Quietly fixes the "100 staff for ~20
clients" optics and closes the economics (the math works if the marquee clients are huge). *Done
when:* one such line exists early in Book 1.

### ☐ Leadership roles made legible via Jay (Ian = CFO+CIO; Leo delegates)
*Ref: `company-audit.md` §2 & §3.*

Make two settled facts *land on the page* through Jay's observation (doubles as characterization):
1. **Ian holds a combined CFO + CIO role** — keep the "CFO" title, but let Jay notice this "CFO" has
   far more client/investment work than expected. Lean on the existing anchors: Luc's explainer
   (B1 ch03), the "CFO's assistant is a client-facing position" line (B1 ch06), Ian rarely attending
   meetings himself (B1 ch16).
2. **Leo's actual remit** — technically the client work is Leo's department, but Leo **delegates the
   execution to Ian** and keeps the schmoozing he enjoys; Ian can schmooze (and has) but prefers and
   excels at the spreadsheet work. Three-beat read: Jay's surprise → "it's really Leo's job" → "Ian
   does it better and Leo lets him." (`characters.md` "CEO" label already corrected.)

Touch: light, 2–3 beats woven into existing scenes. *Done when:* a reader can tell what Ian and Leo
actually do without an info-dump.

### ☐ Jay half-clocks the apartment/mortgage subsidy
*Ref: `book2-audit.md` §3.1 (intentional worldbuilding — Ian's hidden influence).*

The below-market apartment and favorable mortgage are deliberate (institutions give Ian disadvantageous
terms to court his influence; the outcast who's secretly load-bearing). The one lever that makes them
airtight: let **finance-literate Jay half-notice** the terms are too good — clock it, attribute it to
Ian's pull, and let it go — rather than the narrative passing them off as normal. A single beat in the
apartment arrangement and/or the mortgage scene (B2 ch35). *Done when:* the favorable terms are
acknowledged in-text as unusual, not presented as market-rate.

### ☐ Crunch: make the cause explicit + grumbling-under-loyalty
*Ref: `company-audit.md` §5.*

The sleeping-bag crunch is intentional and **bounded** — a one-off disaster from **Ian over-expanding
the client list without expanding headcount** (he needs distraction, dislikes hiring, knows it's
unwise), then remedied (Ray/Liv). Reinforce two things during editing so it reads as abnormal, not the
firm's normal rhythm: (a) the **cause** is Ian's deliberate-but-unwise choice; (b) **team grumbling**
through the worst cycles, anchored by high loyalty — they bear it because the culture's good and the
comp absurd, but clearly wouldn't forever. Makes the eventual hiring/mitigation land harder. *Done
when:* the overload is visibly Ian's doing and visibly temporary.

### ☐ (optional) Finance texture upgrades
*Ref: `finance-primer.md` §4; `company-audit.md` §5.*

Low-priority flavor that makes the *normal* baseline feel authentic (so the abnormal Ian-induced crunch
stands out): let a quarterly crunch be *both* client reporting **and** earnings-season research at once;
ground "strategy season" as the firm's **annual strategic asset-allocation / outlook review** (a real
ritual) rather than an industry-wide season. Optional; skip if it adds clutter.
